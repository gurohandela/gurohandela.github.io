  • Whereabouts

    • Guro, 26 y/o. Always on the look for the reflection of the sun. Know how to code and how to find solutions. Have a deep passion for coffee, collages and hikes. And drawing.

    I have a bachelor degree from University of Oslo. (Informatics: Design, use and interaction). I know do my Msc in interaction design, and I did my equivalient to a minor in social anthropology.

    In social anthropology, I took classes that emphasised our global troubles (the global warming & the linear idea of development). I truly believe that the anthropology perspective helps me understand culture and contexts, making me a good interaction desiger.

    My field of interest is Sustainable Design and Information Systems research. I find the Transition Design Methodology very intriguing, as it uses systems theory very actively.

    I was part of the Research track in 2020-2021. Here we did a research study a research study on whether a online help desk for the users of our worlds biggest free and open source health information software (Dhis2) was a Community of Practice. In January 2023 I travelled to Malawi with the same group of researchers. We looked at whether the use of scannable codes in a EPR system was beneficial. We presented our work at the DSA2023 Conference in Reading, England, in June 2023.

    From August 2021 to June 2023 I lived my life as a cafemanager at Trevarefabrikken in Henningsvær, Lofoten. This included doing some graphical design, drawing classes, one musical performance, event management and hiring.

    I am currently living in my converted VW Transporter (T5). This brings me closer to nature, and is a definite way towards being solution oriented.
